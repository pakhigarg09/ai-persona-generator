AI Persona & Journey Assistant

Problem Statement: During the early discovery phase, UX and product teams spend excessive time defining initial user assumptions. Staring at a blank page to draft "alignment personas" and baseline journey maps slows down momentum and delays actual user testing.

Target Audience: UX Designers, Product Managers, and UX Researchers.

The Solution: An internal AI tool that takes basic product parameters and instantly generates structured, assumptions-based UX personas and journey maps using LLM predictive analytics.

Business Impact: Reduces the time required to draft baseline UX artifacts from several hours to approximately 30 seconds. This allows teams to quickly establish a shared vision, focus their energy on validating these AI-generated assumptions through real user interviews, and accelerate the product development lifecycle.

Before/After Scenario:

Before: A UX designer spends half a day gathering secondary research, drafting fictional backgrounds, and formatting journey map stages on a whiteboard to present to stakeholders.

After: The designer inputs the core product value proposition into the AI Assistant, instantly exports a formatted baseline, and uses the saved hours to recruit actual users to validate the assumptions.
